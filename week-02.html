How Americans are using AI at work, according to a new Gallup poll

Source: Associated Press (January 2026)

← Back to Weekly Articles

Summary

A Gallup survey of more than 22,000 U.S. workers suggests AI is already embedded in the workplace, but adoption is uneven. About 12 percent of workers report using AI tools daily, and roughly one quarter use them at least several times a week. Usage is higher in white-collar industries where AI supports drafting, summarizing, analysis, and admin automation.

Many workers do not see AI as an immediate job threat. However, clerical and administrative roles may be more exposed, especially where training and digital skill development lag. The big theme is not instant job elimination, it is productivity separation between people and teams who adopt AI effectively and those who do not.

Key takeaways

  • AI usage is real today, but concentrated by role and industry.
  • Most workers expect workflow change more than immediate job loss.
  • Training access is the dividing line for who benefits.

Construction and facilities relevance

  • Strong early wins are documentation, scope summaries, meeting notes, and reporting.
  • The risk is getting outpaced by someone who uses AI plus domain knowledge.
  • Firms should standardize guardrails and training to avoid sloppy outputs.

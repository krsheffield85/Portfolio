How Americans are using AI at work, according to a new Gallup poll

Associated Press, January 2026

← Back to Weekly Articles

Summary

A new Gallup survey of more than 22,000 U.S. workers shows that artificial intelligence is already embedded in the modern workplace, but adoption is uneven. About 12 percent of workers report using AI tools daily, and roughly one quarter use them at least several times a week. Usage is highest in white-collar fields such as technology and finance, where employees rely on AI for tasks like drafting emails, summarizing documents, analyzing data, and automating routine administrative work.

Despite growing adoption, many workers do not yet see AI as an immediate threat to their jobs. Only about half of respondents believe AI will significantly affect job security in the next five years. However, the article highlights concern that clerical and administrative workers, especially older employees, may be more vulnerable because they are less likely to receive AI training or have transferable digital skills.

Overall, the findings suggest that AI is reshaping how work is performed rather than eliminating jobs outright, while also raising concerns about workforce inequality, access to training, and the risk that some groups of workers may be left behind as AI adoption accelerates.

Superintelligent AI: Should Its Development Be Stopped?

Source: House of Lords Library (UK Parliament), January 26, 2026

← Back to Weekly Articles

Summary

The briefing discusses Artificial Superintelligence (ASI), defined as a future AI system that would exceed human intelligence across nearly all cognitive domains. It notes that neither AGI nor ASI exist today, but the public debate has intensified as capability improves. Timelines are uncertain and experts disagree.

The potential benefits are huge, like breakthroughs in medicine, climate modeling, and complex problem-solving. Risks are equally serious, including loss of control, misuse, concentration of power, and alignment failures where systems act outside human goals. The briefing highlights governance, regulation, and international coordination as key needs.

Key takeaways

  • ASI is not here, but governance problems need solving before capability outruns control.
  • Timelines are uncertain, stakes are massive.
  • Safety research and international coordination are central themes.

Construction and facilities relevance

  • Safety-critical industries need audit trails, verification, and accountability baked in.
  • “Guardrails first” matters now, even for narrow AI.
  • Vendor lock-in and data ownership are already real versions of power concentration.
